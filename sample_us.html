5.0 out of 5 starsmisty blue and lilac too
ByBecke D.on March 20, 2000
Format: Audio CD
This is Brian May's glorious sophomore solo album, and those who know his first album know what to expect: some darn fine music. "Another World" starts out with the embryonic "Space", which then launches into the rockin' "Business", a tormented yet beautiful plea for peace. "China Belle", next, sports one wicked guitar, as well as some rather predictable lyrics. Number four, the breathtaking "Why Don't We Try Again", is my personal favorite. Anyone who doubts Brian's worthiness as a vocalist need only listen to this song once to be convinced of the folly of their ways. Next comes "On My Way Up". From the first 'heyyy' it's fun, it's flip, it's Brian being adorable as always. The metallic "Cyborg" shows off some of Brian's supernatural guitar talent. Wow. Moving on, there's "The Guv'nor" (with Jeff Beck). Okay: All I can say is, here are two of the best guitarists in rock history, working together. What do YOU think the result is going to be like? It's mayhem. It's a long way from the next song, the searchingly sentimental "Wilderness". Then it's back to mad mad rock'n'roll with the excellent cover of the Beatles tune "Slow Down". This is followed by another worthy cover, Jimi Hendrix's "One Rainy Wish", which Brian executes with flair and power. Well done, indeed. Pressing on, there's more good stuff: the live-effect "All the Way From Memphis", a cheeky Mott the Hoople cover (with a guest appearance by Ian Hunter himself). Now it's time to wind down. The last song on the album is the title track, "Another World". (Leave it to Brian to put the title track last.) It's a lovely piece, with beautifully wistful lyrics and sweetly gentle acoustic guitar work. Rewarding the patient listener, the false ending leads to an instrumental reprise of "Business", to conclude the album on a soft, meditative note. This is a must have for all Brian and Queen fans, as well as for anyone else who truly loves music. Mightily recommended.
